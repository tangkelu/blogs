# Canonical Summary

> The current source-backed path for transparent, stretchable, and biodegradable PCB drafts is named material-system framing, not generic claims that ordinary PCB factories can build transparent, stretchable, or biodegradable multilayer boards.

## Stable Facts

- Corning describes Willow Glass as a thin, flexible glass substrate for ultra-slim displays and states that it can be processed at temperatures up to `500 C`.
- Corning positions Willow Glass around displays, smart surfaces, touch sensors, OLED / LCD backplanes and color filters, and conformable or curved display use cases.
- Henkel describes LOCTITE ECI 1501 E&C as a formable conductive silver ink for 3D-shaped circuits and In-Molded Electronics, compatible with polyester, polycarbonate, and some TPU foils.
- Henkel states LOCTITE ECI 1501 E&C is suitable for screen printing and gives a possible elongation-to-break value of `50%`.
- Qnity describes Activegrid inks and films as having optoelectronic properties and flexibility for capacitive touch interfaces, In-Molded Electronics, smart windows, and displays.
- Jiva describes Soluboard CCL as compatible with etching, electroplating, drilling, routing, and other standard PCB fabrication processes.
- Jiva states Soluboard contains natural fibres, a water-soluble polymer, and a halogen-free flame retardant, and that it has achieved PTH compatibility while retaining hot-water end-of-life breakdown behavior.
- Jiva publishes source-scoped comparison figures for Soluboard, including `5.52 kg` carbon footprint per square metre versus `16.94 kg` for FR-4, `67%` reduction overall, `1.35 g/cm3` density versus `2 g/cm3` for FR-4, and `590 g` plastic saved per square metre compared to FR-4.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this fact for transparent PCB, stretchable PCB, biodegradable PCB, printed electronics, smart-window, IME, wearable-material, or sustainability article drafts.
- Preserve exact product names, vendor context, units, and stated conditions for every value.
- Treat Soluboard environmental values as vendor-published, source-scoped estimates, not universal lifecycle values for every board design.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove that glass, transparent films, conductive inks, or Soluboard can replace FR-4 in every PCB stackup.
- It does not prove multilayer transparent PCB fabrication, plated transparent vias, solder-assembly compatibility, optical transmission, haze, sheet resistance, fatigue cycle life, medical skin safety, automotive qualification, compostability certification, or field lifetime.
- It does not prove any HILPCB / APTPCB / Highleap manufacturing capability, yield, quality rate, stock, lead time, MOQ, or certification.

## Open Questions
