# Canonical Summary

> `85N` is an official Arlon exact-product high-temperature polyimide anchor with datasheet-backed numeric values that must stay condition-bound.

## Stable Facts

- Arlon publishes an official product page, datasheet, and processing guide for `85N`.
- The source posture is strong enough to treat `85N` as an exact-product anchor rather than a family-only mention.
- The official product page places `85N` in the high-temperature polyimide branch.
- `Tg (TMA)` is `>=250 C`.
- `Td initial` is `387 C`.
- `Td at 5% weight loss` is `407 C`.
- `Dk` is `4.2 @ 1 MHz` and `4.0 @ 1 GHz`.
- `Df` is `0.01 @ 1 MHz`; `Df @ 1 GHz` is `N/A`.
- `CTE (X,Y)` is `16 ppm/C`.
- `CTE (Z)` is `<Tg 55 ppm/C` and `>Tg 149 ppm/C`.
- `Z-axis expansion` is `1.2%` from `50-260 C`.
- `water absorption` is `0.27%` at `0.062"`.
- `peel strength to copper` is `6.4 lb/in (1.1 N/mm)` with the datasheet's stated conditions.
- The official wording includes `compatible with lead-free processing`, `RoHS/WEEE compliant`, and `meets IPC-4101/40 and /41 description and specification`.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep every numeric value tied to the official `85N` datasheet conditions and wording.
- Keep the multiple peel-strength conditions separate if you later expand the card.
- Treat the processing guide as guarded process-discipline context only.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not flatten `Df` into a single cross-frequency value.
- It does not combine the peel-strength conditions into one unconditional number.
- It does not turn compliance wording into a static guarantee without refresh.
- It does not replace design-specific thermal, reliability, or assembly validation.
