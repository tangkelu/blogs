# Canonical Summary

> `14-layer` flex-material supplementation can now use narrow exact-product anchors where product identity and stated conditions are preserved, especially Panasonic `R-F705S` and Arlon `85NT` as separate product-grade references. This does not authorize generic `polyimide`, `LCP`, `FRCC`, or rigid-flex material numeric tables, and it does not unlock bend or fabrication claims.

## Stable Facts

- A conservative `14-layer` rewrite may keep flex-material discussion at class level and selectively add exact-product examples when the product identity is explicit.
- Panasonic's FELIOS sources support a narrow exact-product `LCP` exception through `R-F705S` with stated methods and conditions.
- Panasonic's FELIOS family sources also support broader class-level flex-material discussion without turning one row into a universal flex-material table.
- Arlon's official `85NT` product page, datasheet, and processing guide support a product-grade polyimide branch anchor rather than only a generic family mention.
- `R-F705S` and `85NT` should remain separate product-grade examples and must not be merged into one mixed rigid-flex material ladder.
- These sources support wording such as `exact-product example`, `class-level family framing`, and `conditions stay attached to the cited product row`.
- These sources do not support generic `polyimide`, `Kapton`, `UPILEX`, `adhesiveless flex`, `FRCC`, or rigid-flex reliability numerics as reusable defaults.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card when a `14-layer` draft wants one or two exact-product material examples without pretending the entire flex-material branch is numerically covered.

## Limits And Non-Claims
