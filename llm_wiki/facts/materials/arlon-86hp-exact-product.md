# Canonical Summary

> `86HP` is an official Arlon exact-product high-performance polyimide anchor with current datasheet-backed numeric values that must stay condition-bound.

## Stable Facts

- Arlon publishes an official product page and a current `86HP v1.6` datasheet for `86HP`.
- The source posture is strong enough to treat `86HP` as an exact-product anchor rather than a family-only mention.
- The product page identifies `86HP` as a high-performance polyimide branch.
- The datasheet states `Tg >=250 C`.
- The datasheet states `decomposition temperature >430 C`.
- The datasheet states `T300 >60 minutes`.
- The datasheet states Z-axis expansion is `<=1%` between `50-260 C`.
- The datasheet states thermal conductivity is `0.6 W/mK`.
- The datasheet states `Dk/Df` is `3.5/0.005 @ 1 GHz`.
- The datasheet states anti-CAF performance passes `1000 hours` at `85% RH / 85 C`.
- The datasheet states `86HP` meets the requirements of `IPC-4101/40/41`.
- The datasheet wording includes `halogen-free`, `compatible with lead-free processing`, `meets UL-94 V0`, and `RoHS/WEEE compliant`.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep every numeric value tied to the official `86HP v1.6` datasheet wording and conditions.
- Treat application wording for aerospace, industrial, military, HDI, or high-layer-count use as manufacturer positioning, not universal qualification proof.
- Re-check the datasheet revision before using `86HP` in comparison tables or recommendation content.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove a specific stackup, drill-life outcome, or sequential-lamination yield in a given fab flow.
- It does not turn datasheet or product-page wording into automatic aerospace, military, or space qualification status.
- It does not close the unresolved Arlon RF/PTFE family gap for `CLTE-XT`, `TC350`, `AD250`, `AD255`, `AD300`, `CuClad`, or `DiClad`.
