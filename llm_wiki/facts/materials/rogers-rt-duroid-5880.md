# Canonical Summary

> RT/duroid 5880 is a Rogers PTFE composite laminate positioned for extremely low-loss microwave and millimeter-wave applications where insertion loss matters more than FR-4-style processing convenience.

## Stable Facts

- Rogers identifies RT/duroid 5880 as a glass microfiber reinforced PTFE composite.
- The official product page and datasheet list `Dk 2.20 +/- 0.02` and `Df 0.0009 at 10 GHz`.
- The datasheet lists thermal conductivity `0.20 W/m/K`, moisture absorption `0.02%`, specific gravity `2.2`, and peel strength `34 lb/in` for 1 oz electrodeposited copper.
- Rogers positions RT/duroid 5880 as suitable for broadband applications through millimeter-wave frequencies because of its low dielectric constant and low loss.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep woven-glass or microfiber PTFE framing separate from RO3000 ceramic-filled PTFE framing.
- Use the datasheet when quoting numeric values and the product page for application positioning.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not mean RT/duroid 5880 is automatically the best choice for every high-frequency PCB.
- Ultra-low loss does not remove the need to consider dimensional stability, thermal path, manufacturability, and stackup complexity.
