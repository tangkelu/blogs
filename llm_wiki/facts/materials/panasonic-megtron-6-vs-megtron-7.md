# Canonical Summary

> In the current public Panasonic grades captured in this wiki, MEGTRON 7 reads as the lower-loss, higher-heat variant relative to MEGTRON 6, while both remain firmly in the process-friendly high-speed multilayer category rather than the soft PTFE ultra-low-loss category.

## Stable Facts

- The public R-5775(N) MEGTRON 6 grade lists `Tg 185 C`, while the public R-5785(N) MEGTRON 7 grade lists `Tg 200 C`.
- The public MEGTRON 6 grade lists `Df 0.004` at `12 GHz`, while the public MEGTRON 7 grade lists `Df 0.002` at `12 GHz`.
- The public MEGTRON 6 grade lists water absorption `0.14%`, while the public MEGTRON 7 grade lists `0.06%`.
- Both public grades list `94V-0` flammability and `T288 >120 min`.
- Panasonic positions MEGTRON 7 more explicitly around very high-speed servers, routers, and large data volumes.

## Conditions And Methods

- This comparison is currently based on the public N grades captured in the registry.
- Do not generalize these exact values to every grade in the broader families without checking the corresponding model page or datasheet.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This is not a full family-to-family characterization.
- It does not compare copper profile, channel-loss performance, or fabricator-specific process yield.
