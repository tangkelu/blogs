# Canonical Summary

> N7000-3 is an AGC toughened polyimide laminate and prepreg system positioned for severe-condition multilayers, fine-geometry surface-mount / BGA builds, avionics, down-well petroleum, and burn-in board contexts, but its values must stay separate from N7000-3F.

## Stable Facts

- AGC describes N7000-3 as a toughened polyimide laminate and prepreg.
- The TDS frames applications around backplanes, fine-line surface-mount and BGA multilayers, avionics, down-well petroleum, and burn-in boards.
- The TDS states N7000-3 uses a next-generation high-Tg polyimide system with toughened resin chemistry.
- The TDS lists `Dk 3.5 at 1 GHz` by `IPC-TM-650 2.5.5.9`.
- The same TDS lists `Dk 3.5 at 10 GHz` by `IPC-TM-650 2.5.5.5`.
- The same TDS lists `Df 0.009 at 2.5 GHz` and `Df 0.009 at 10 GHz`.
- The same TDS lists Tg `260 C` by DSC and `IPC-TM-650 2.4.25c`.
- The same TDS lists degradation temperature `376 C` at `5%` weight loss and `T-260 120+ minutes`.
- The same TDS lists thermal conductivity `0.45 W/mK` by `ASTM E1461`.
- The same TDS lists X/Y CTE from `-40 C to +125 C` of `9 / 12 ppm/C` and Z-axis expansion `< 2.5%` for `43% RC` from `50 C to 260 C`.
- The same TDS lists moisture absorption `0.35 wt.%`.
- The TDS states N7000-3 meets `UL 94V-1` and `IPC-4101/40`, `/41`, and `/42`, with UL file number `E36295`.
- The TDS states N7000-3 can be manufactured in laminate thickness from `2 mil / 0.05 mm` and up.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep `1 GHz`, `2.5 GHz`, and `10 GHz` electrical values separate.
- Keep N7000-3 and N7000-3F separate; do not substitute one product's Dk/Df, expansion, or peel-strength values for the other.
- Treat avionics, down-well petroleum, burn-in board, and severe-condition language as manufacturer material-positioning context unless separate qualification or test evidence is attached.
- Treat the values as typical datasheet values, not guaranteed specification limits.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove finished-board avionics, petroleum, burn-in, or high-temperature qualification.
- It does not prove a generic multilayer recipe, lamination count, stackup success, or plated-through-hole reliability for a finished design.
- It does not prove APT, HIL, or any other supplier capability, certification, yield, lead time, or qualification status.
