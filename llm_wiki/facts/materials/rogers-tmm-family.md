# Canonical Summary

> Rogers TMM materials are thermoset microwave laminates intended to combine ceramic-like electrical and mechanical stability with easier mechanical processing than very soft PTFE laminates.

## Stable Facts

- The TMM family datasheet covers TMM 3, TMM 4, TMM 6, TMM 10, TMM 10i, and TMM 13i.
- Rogers states TMM laminates offer low moisture absorption, low thermal coefficient of dielectric constant, and lower thermal expansion than typical PTFE/glass materials.
- The family spans dielectric constants from roughly `3.27` for TMM 3 up to `12.85` for TMM 13i.
- The family datasheet lists thermal conductivity improving significantly in the `i` variants, with TMM 10i shown at `2.4 W/m.K`.
- Rogers states TMM laminates are dimensionally stable and suitable for plated-through-hole constructions.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep family-level claims separate from member-specific values.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This family card is not a substitute for selecting a specific TMM grade.
- It does not prove a TMM material is lower loss than RT/duroid 5880.
