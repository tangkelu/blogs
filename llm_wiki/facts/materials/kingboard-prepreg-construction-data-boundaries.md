# Canonical Summary

> Kingboard prepreg and construction data can be used only when the exact prepreg family, glass style, resin content, pressed thickness, dielectric frequency, and source are preserved. It must not be averaged into one Kingboard or FR-4 dielectric number.

## Stable Facts

- Recovered KBLaminates source records include prepreg-paired systems such as `KB-6060`, `KB-6064`, `KB-6065`, `KB-6065F`, `KB-6067F`, `PP-HF140`, `PP-HF170`, `PP-KB3200G`, `PP-PI520G`, `KB-6060A`, `KB-6060F`, `KB-6065C`, `KB-6065LE`, `KB-6067GMD`, `KB-6067GLD`, `KB-6068LE`, `KB-6069GT`, and `PP-PI515G`.
- Prepreg Dk / Df, resin content, and pressed thickness are construction-sensitive.
- A blog may cite prepreg construction only when the exact product, prepreg family, glass style, resin content, frequency, and official source are present.
- A selector may say that construction data exists for a recovered family, but must not flatten construction tables into one universal value.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep laminate and prepreg names paired as published by KBLaminates.
- Prefer exact construction tables when stackup or impedance discussion depends on dielectric behavior.
- If a draft only needs material-family positioning, use the exact-product laminate fact instead of pulling construction rows into the article.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not authorize one generic Kingboard Dk / Df number.
- It does not authorize generic FR-4 averages.
- It does not authorize stackup recipes, impedance guarantees, resin-flow recipes, lamination-cycle promises, or HIL/APT process capability.
- It does not allow suffix inheritance across similar product names.
