# Canonical Summary

> N4000-13 SI is an AGC high-speed multifunctional epoxy laminate and prepreg system positioned for signal-integrity and controlled-impedance designs that need lower Dk/Df than conventional FR-4 while keeping high-Tg FR-4-style processing context.

## Stable Facts

- AGC describes N4000-13 SI as a high-speed multifunctional epoxy laminate and prepreg.
- The TDS states N4000-13 SI uses `SI` glass for applications requiring signal integrity and precise impedance control.
- The TDS frames applications around high-speed storage networks, internet switches / routing systems, wireless communication infrastructure, and backplanes.
- For `50% resin content`, the TDS lists `Dk 3.4 at 2.5 GHz` by Split Post Cavity and `Dk 3.2 at 10 GHz` by Stripline, with `IPC-TM-650 2.5.5.5` shown for the property row.
- For `50% resin content`, the same TDS lists `Df 0.008 at 2.5 GHz` by Split Post Cavity and `Df 0.008 at 10 GHz` by Stripline.
- The same TDS lists Tg `240 C` by DMA `Tan d Peak`.
- The same TDS lists degradation temperature `350 C` at `5%` weight loss, `T-260 30+ minutes`, and `T-288 10+ minutes`.
- The same TDS lists thermal conductivity `0.294 W/mK` by `ASTM E1461`.
- The same TDS lists X/Y CTE from `-40 C to +125 C` of `10 / 14 ppm/C`, Z-axis CTE Alpha 1 `70 ppm/C`, Z-axis CTE Alpha 2 `280 ppm/C`, and Z-axis expansion `3.5%`.
- The same TDS lists moisture absorption `0.1 wt.%`.
- The TDS states N4000-13 SI meets `UL 94V-0` and `IPC-4101/29`, `/98`, `/99`, and `/101`, with UL file number `E36295`.
- The TDS states N4000-13 SI can be manufactured in laminate thickness from `2 mil / 0.05 mm` and up.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep the `50% resin content` condition attached to the Dk/Df values.
- Keep the `2.5 GHz Split Post Cavity` and `10 GHz Stripline` values separate.
- Treat lead-free assembly compatibility as design-dependent, matching the datasheet note.
- Treat the values as typical datasheet values, not guaranteed specification limits.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove a finished board meets an impedance tolerance, insertion-loss target, backplane protocol budget, or high-speed channel requirement.
- It does not prove lead-free assembly success for any specific stackup, since the datasheet states compatibility is design dependent.
- It does not prove APT, HIL, or any other supplier capability, certification, yield, lead time, or qualification status.
