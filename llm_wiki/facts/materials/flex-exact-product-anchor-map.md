# Canonical Summary

> The flex corpus now has narrow exact-product anchors for `UPILEX-S`, `Kapton HN`, `85N`, `85NT`, `N7000-3F`, and Panasonic `R-F705S` as an adjacent LCP exception. These are useful for source-scoped material-language recovery, but they do not authorize generic bend-life, bend-radius, rigid-flex, or finished-board reliability claims.

## Stable Facts

- UBE's official pages anchor `UPILEX-S` as a product-grade polyimide film.
- DuPont's official product page and data sheet anchor `Kapton HN` as an all-polyimide film.
- Arlon's official sources anchor `85N` as a high-temperature polyimide product grade.
- Arlon's official sources anchor `85NT` as a polyimide nonwoven-aramid product grade.
- AGC's official datasheet anchors `N7000-3F` as a toughened filled polyimide laminate and prepreg system.
- Panasonic's official FELIOS LCP sources anchor `R-F705S` as a narrow exact-product LCP exception, not a generic PI substitute.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep each product name and source owner attached to its own values and wording.
- Use these anchors only when the draft needs exact-product flex-material identity.
- Preserve generic `polyimide`, `LCP`, and `adhesiveless flex` as separate lanes unless a product source is cited.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove flex-board qualification, bend reliability, stackup selection, or supplier approval.
- It does not convert one product-grade row into generic flex-material capability.
- It does not merge LCP and polyimide into one interchangeable family.
