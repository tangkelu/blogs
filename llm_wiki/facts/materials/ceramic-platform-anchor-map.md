# Canonical Summary

> The ceramic lane now has official anchors for `LTCC`, thin-film ceramic circuits, ceramic substrates, `AlN`, and Ventec `IMS` family wording. MARUWA's AlN page also exposes process-family names such as `thin film`, `thick film`, `DBC`, `AMB`, and `DPC`, but these remain family names rather than universal capability proof.

## Stable Facts

- KYOCERA defines `LTCC` as low-temperature co-fired ceramics and frames it as glass ceramics.
- KYOCERA thin-film pages anchor ceramic thin-film circuit vocabulary, including vacuum deposition and sputtering on ceramic substrates.
- CeramTec anchors ceramic substrates as a class that includes alumina and aluminum nitride.
- MARUWA anchors `AlN` as a substrate family and lists `thin film`, `thick film`, `DBC`, `AMB`, and `DPC` as processing-family context.
- Ventec's IMS family page provides an official `IMS` / metal-base-laminate family anchor.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep `LTCC`, `thin-film ceramic`, `IMS`, `DBC`, `AMB`, and `DPC` separate unless a source explicitly connects them.
- Use the source-owner wording for each family and avoid vendor-neutral process tables.
- Attach product datasheets before quoting thermal, dielectric, thickness, metallization, or layer-structure numbers.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove a universal ceramic-platform capability table.
- It does not validate HIL/APT ceramic manufacturing limits, hermeticity, or stackup rules.
- It does not claim `DBC` or `AMB` availability from every ceramic supplier.
