# Canonical Summary

> KYOCERA's official thin-film pages support real process vocabulary for thin-film circuit boards: conductive and insulating layers can be formed on substrate surfaces using vacuum deposition technology; ceramic thin-film processing can support finer, higher-precision patterns than screen-printed thick-film technology in KYOCERA's framing; and ceramic examples include `Al2O3`, `AlN`, and `Si3N4`. These sources do not support universal line/space, conductor-thickness, RF, medical, aerospace, or supplier-capability claims.

## Stable Facts

- KYOCERA describes thin-film circuit boards as boards where conductive and insulating layers are formed on a substrate surface using vacuum deposition technology.
- KYOCERA lists process examples including vacuum deposition and sputtering.
- KYOCERA describes thin-film circuit boards as generally used where high-precision circuitry is required.
- KYOCERA states that applying thin-film processing to ceramics can provide finer, higher-precision patterns than screen-printed thick-film technology in multilayer ceramic structures.
- KYOCERA lists ceramic material examples for thin-film processing including `Al2O3`, `AlN`, and `Si3N4`.
- KYOCERA lists example thin-film metal stacks such as `Ti-Pt-Au`, `Ti-Pd-Au`, `Ti-Al`, and copper / nickel / palladium / gold-containing stacks.
