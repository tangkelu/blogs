# Canonical Summary

> MEGTRON 7 is a Panasonic ultra-low-loss, highly heat-resistant multilayer PCB material family positioned for very high-speed networking and large-data-volume hardware rather than as a general-purpose microwave PTFE substitute.

## Stable Facts

- Panasonic states the ultra-low dielectric constant and dissipation factor of MEGTRON 7 make it suitable for servers and routers associated with 5G-scale data volumes.
- Panasonic also states the MEGTRON 7 family is HDI compatible with very high layer count and large-format PCB layouts.
- For the public R-5785(N) / R-5680(N) grade, Panasonic lists `Tg (DSC) 200 C`, `Td 400 C`, `T288 >120 min`, and flammability `94V-0`.
- The same public model page lists `Dk 3.4` at `1 GHz` and `12 GHz`, with `Df 0.001` at `1 GHz` and `0.002` at `12 GHz`.
- The same page lists water absorption `0.06%` and thermal conductivity `0.40 W/m.K`.

## Conditions And Methods

- Keep the specific grade identifier with the values.
- Treat MEGTRON 7 primarily as a high-speed / high-layer-count low-loss multilayer option unless a specific RF design requirement justifies broader interpretation.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not claim MEGTRON 7 is the right default for every RF or antenna board.
- It also does not prove one public grade represents all MEGTRON 7 variants.
