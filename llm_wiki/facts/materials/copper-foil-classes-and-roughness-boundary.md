# Canonical Summary

> Public IPC metadata and official copper-foil vendor pages support copper-foil class vocabulary such as electrodeposited foil, wrought / rolled foil, foil profile, grade, and bond-enhancement treatment. They do not support universal roughness defaults, RF-loss rankings, bend-life claims, or heavy-copper current / heat performance claims.

## Stable Facts

- The public `IPC-4562B` TOC supports copper-foil category vocabulary including foil type, foil grade, foil profile, bond-enhancement treatment, electrodeposited foil, wrought foil, thickness, peel strength, and treatment integrity.
- JX provides official product-family pages for electro-deposited copper foil and rolled copper foil.
- JX's rolled-foil page supports source-owner-specific framing that rolled copper foil is distinct from electro-deposited foil and can be positioned around lower roughness / flexibility contexts.
- Tex Technology provides an official rolled-copper-foil page that supports rolling / annealing process-route vocabulary and flexibility positioning.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card when drafts discuss `RA`, rolled annealed foil, ED foil, low-profile foil, HVLP foil, copper roughness, RF loss, flex circuits, or foil profile.
- Keep class-level language separate from exact roughness and insertion-loss values.
- Attach exact product datasheets and test conditions before quoting `Ra`, `Rz`, peel strength, Dk / Df impact, skin-effect loss, bend life, current capacity, or thermal results.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not support a universal claim that rolled foil is always lower loss, more flexible, or better than ED foil.
- It does not provide copper roughness values, RF insertion-loss numbers, bend-cycle limits, foil thickness tolerances, or bond-treatment performance.
- It does not prove supplier copper availability, stackup compatibility, lamination process capability, lead time, or cost.
