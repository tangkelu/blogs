# Canonical Summary

> A CT detector-array board is safest when rewritten as a medical-imaging board-review article focused on detector-chain identity, readout and interface ownership, hidden-joint inspection, HDI / via-geometry restraint, and layered validation. The current evidence layer does not support generic cost-optimization tables, detector-performance proof, dose claims, or clinical outcomes.

## Stable Facts

- NIBIB supports `computed tomography` as a public CT identity anchor in the medical-imaging domain.
- Vendor CT pages can safely supply detector-family identity only when paired with the public medical-imaging anchor.
- The broad medical boundary page supports CT writing only at board-class and manufacturing-control level.
- The hidden-joint inspection boundary supports X-ray / CT as a concealed-joint visibility lane, not as universal acceptance proof.
- The via-geometry and HDI boundary supports route and build-up complexity language, but not universal drill, yield, or capability proof.
- The FAI / final-inspection boundary supports layered release language around build confirmation and inspection gates.
- The BOM / HDI boundary supports complexity-reduction language as a process-boundary topic, not as a price table.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card when a draft says `CT detector array`, `CT detector board`, or similar language but the safest rewrite is still a release-review article.
- Keep detector-family identity at board-chain level only.
- Keep hidden-joint inspection, HDI complexity, and validation as separate review surfaces.
- Treat any pricing or cost language as complexity posture, not a savings promise.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not support detector sensitivity, resolution, dose, image-quality, or clinical-performance claims.
- It does not support compliance proof, qualification proof, or medical-device approval claims.
- It does not support universal layer-count, via, finish, or cost-ranking tables.
- It does not support yield, scrap, lead-time, or market-price claims.
