# Canonical Summary

> The `/code/blogs/tmps/2025.10.1/en` drafts can be consumed as a service taxonomy and source-gap map for PCB fabrication, assembly, prototype, production, engineering, testing, inspection, repair, rework, quote, supplier, copying, cloning, replication, and reverse-engineering topics. They do not prove commercial numbers, supplier capability, certification status, factory capacity, turnaround promises, legal permission, IP safety, quality outcomes, or application readiness.

## Stable Facts

- The batch contains 28 temporary English PCB service drafts.
- Existing `llm_wiki` support can route prototype, quick-turn, turnkey, component sourcing, SMT/THT assembly, AOI, X-ray, ICT, FCT, sourcing, traceability, NPI, and release-gate language through source-backed internal service records and method cards.
- Existing high-reliability and FDA/eCFR metadata supports guarded supplier-control, traceability, and documentation vocabulary, but only as vocabulary boundaries.
- Current `llm_wiki` does not contain an official legal-source layer for board copying, cloning, replication, reverse engineering, repair rights, IP ownership, or customer authorization.
- The matching ingestion map is `logs/p4-34-2025-10-1-commercial-service-and-legal-sensitive-blog-ingestion-map.md`.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card only as a prompt-consumption gate for the temporary `2025.10.1` blog drafts.
- For fabrication, assembly, prototype, production, inspection, testing, sourcing, NPI, and rework wording, route claims to existing `llm_wiki` facts and wiki pages before writing.
- For quote, cost, price, lead time, MOQ, delivery, stock, and production-scale wording, use the drafts only to identify buyer questions and source gaps.
- For supplier, factory, company, manufacturer, and `why choose us` wording, require dated capability records or official non-blog source records before making reusable claims.
- For copying, cloning, replication, and reverse-engineering topics, require explicit legal-source recovery and customer-authorization boundaries before making any legal, ethical, or IP-safe claim.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not add pricing, cost-share, benchmark, MOQ, quote-turnaround, lead-time, delivery, stock, yield, DPPM, quality-rate, throughput, or savings data.
- It does not prove HILPCB, Highleap, or APTPCB factory capacity, certifications, equipment, process limits, engineering depth, production scale, inspection coverage, or customer outcomes.
- It does not prove BGA rework, microsection, X-ray, FAI, AOI, ICT, FCT, reverse-engineering, redesign, legacy upgrade, or emergency modification capability beyond already sourced boundary language.
- It does not establish that copying, cloning, replication, or reverse engineering is legal, ethical, IP-safe, or commercially permissible in any specific case.
- It does not support automotive, medical, aerospace, defense, 5G, mmWave, industrial, or regulated-market readiness claims from draft prose.

## Open Questions

- Whether to create a commercial-governance source lane for admissible quote, price, lead-time, MOQ, and delivery language.
- Whether HILPCB / Highleap / APT have dated capability records for current certifications, equipment, process windows, quality metrics, and service scope.
- Whether a legal-source lane should be opened for repair rights, customer authorization, reverse engineering, IP ownership, cloning, copying, and spare-board reproduction.
- Whether obsolescence, redesign, and legacy upgrade topics need separate official sources before they become reusable blog data.
