# Canonical Summary

> IPC public TM-650 records provide real method anchors for board-level thermal shock / thermal cycle / continuity, rigid printed-wiring vibration, and metallic-surface solderability. They should be consumed as method vocabulary and scope boundaries, not as reusable industry-wide temperature, cycle, G-level, wetting, shelf-life, or pass/fail tables.

## Stable Facts

- IPC-TM-650 `2.6.7.2C` is an IPC method anchor for thermal shock, thermal cycle, and continuity in printed-board contexts.
- IPC-TM-650 `2.6.9B` is an IPC method anchor for vibration testing of rigid printed wiring.
- IPC-TM-650 `2.4.14` is an IPC method anchor for solderability of metallic surfaces.
- The IPC TM-650 public index supports method naming and discovery but does not by itself provide acceptance criteria.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card when blogs mention thermal cycling, thermal shock, vibration, solderability, surface-finish readiness, or board reliability screening.
- State that test severity and acceptance criteria come from customer requirements, application standards, licensed standard text, drawings, qualification plans, or dated internal procedures.
- Keep board-level IPC method scope separate from component-level JEDEC tests and finished-product regulatory qualification.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not support universal cycle counts, chamber ramp rates, dwell times, temperature extremes, vibration G-levels, PSD profiles, or industry-specific severity tables.
- It does not support finish shelf-life windows, `95%` wetting coverage, contact-angle thresholds, black-pad prevention, or gold-thickness rules.
- It does not prove PCBA burn-in, field-life correlation, automotive qualification, medical suitability, aerospace release, supplier capability, or quality-rate claims.
