# Canonical Summary

> J-STD-001 and IPC-A-610 are useful public metadata anchors for soldered assembly requirements and electronic-assembly acceptability, but their public TOCs do not support clause-level workmanship or process claims.

## Stable Facts

- IPC J-STD-001 is the standard family to anchor requirements for soldered electrical and electronic assemblies.
- IPC-A-610 is the standard family to anchor acceptability of electronic assemblies.
- Public TOC PDFs can establish document identity, broad scope, and section discovery.
- The IPC revision table should be checked before publication when a specific revision label is mentioned.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card for SMT, THT, soldering, inspection, FAI/FQI, and PCBA quality articles when standards are referenced at a high level.
- Pair public TOC metadata with licensed standards or customer specifications before making class-specific acceptance claims.
- Keep internal process capability claims separate from IPC acceptance requirements.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not state solder-joint criteria, cleanliness limits, class-specific acceptance rules, visual defect thresholds, or process-control requirements.
- It does not reproduce IPC standard text.
- It does not prove that the latest revision is J or H; revision labels must be refreshed before publication.

## Open Questions

- Confirm which IPC assembly standards and revisions are licensed internally.
- Decide whether future evidence packs should carry separate cards for J-STD-001 and IPC-A-610 once licensed clause access exists.
