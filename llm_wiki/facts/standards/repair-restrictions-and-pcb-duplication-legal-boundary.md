# Canonical Summary

> The FTC `Nixing the Fix` report is useful policy context for repair restrictions and repair-market concerns, but it is not permission to copy, clone, replicate, or reverse-engineer a PCB. In `llm_wiki`, repair and rework blogs may use this source only for cautious repair-policy context; any IP-sensitive duplication or reverse-engineering claim still needs customer authorization, ownership / license review, contract review, and legal-source support.

## Stable Facts

- The FTC published `Nixing the Fix: An FTC Report to Congress on Repair Restrictions` in May 2021.
- The report is an official U.S. government policy source about repair restrictions and repair-market concerns.
- The report can support careful background language that repair restrictions are a recognized policy and competition topic.
- The report does not by itself establish that a specific PCB copying, cloning, spare-board reproduction, or reverse-engineering project is lawful.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card for `pcb-repair.md`, `pcb-rework.md`, `pcb-modification.md`, and `pcb-upgrade.md` only as repair-policy context.
- For `pcb-copying.md`, `pcb-cloning.md`, `pcb-replication.md`, and `pcb-reverse-engineering.md`, treat the source as a boundary: it explains why repair access can matter, but it does not authorize duplication.
- Require project-specific customer authorization, IP ownership or license evidence, contract review, and legal review before writing legality, ethical permission, or IP-safe service claims.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card is not legal advice.
- It does not authorize copying firmware, schematics, PCB layouts, mask works, software, labels, trademarks, or protected product designs.
- It does not prove that a supplier may lawfully reproduce a board, bypass access controls, extract firmware, clone a controller, or sell spare boards for a third-party product.
- It does not prove HILPCB, Highleap, or APTPCB legal compliance, reverse-engineering capability, repair capability, or customer authorization process.
