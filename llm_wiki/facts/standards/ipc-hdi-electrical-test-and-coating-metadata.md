# Canonical Summary

> IPC-2226, IPC-9252, and IPC-CC-830 can anchor HDI design, unpopulated-board electrical testing, and conformal-coating qualification topics at the metadata level, but public pages do not justify extracting technical limits or pass/fail criteria.

## Stable Facts

- IPC-2226 belongs to IPC's board-design standards context and is used as an HDI printed-board design-standard anchor.
- IPC-9252B is an IPC standard anchor for requirements for electrical testing of unpopulated printed boards.
- IPC-CC-830C is an IPC standard anchor for qualification and performance of electrical insulating compounds for printed wiring assemblies.
- IPC revision metadata is dynamic and should be refreshed before exact revision labels are published.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card to support blog sections that mention HDI/microvia design standards, bare-board electrical test, or conformal-coating standards at a high level.
- For manufacturing rules, test coverage, or coating qualification details, rely on licensed standards, customer specifications, and current engineering process documents.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not provide microvia geometry limits, stackup rules, test thresholds, adjacency rules, coating thickness limits, qualification-test criteria, or pass/fail requirements.
- It does not state that a board or coating is compliant with any IPC standard.
- It does not replace current customer drawing or contract requirements.
