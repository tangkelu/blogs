# Canonical Summary

> LED PCB and MCPCB blogs must not collapse all LED testing into one claim. `LM-79` is the product-level optical/electrical measurement anchor, `LM-80` is the LED-source maintenance-data anchor, `TM-21` is the luminous / photon / radiant flux maintenance projection anchor, and `IEC 62471` is the photobiological-safety anchor for lamps and lamp systems.

## Stable Facts

- `ANSI/IES LM-79-24` is the current official IES store-page anchor observed in this pass for optical and electrical measurements of solid-state lighting products.
- `ANSI/IES LM-80-21` is the official IES store-page anchor for measuring maintenance of light-output characteristics of solid-state light sources.
- `ANSI/IES TM-21-21` is the official IES store-page anchor for projecting long-term luminous, photon, and radiant flux maintenance of LED light sources.
- `IEC 62471:2006` is the official IEC standard-family anchor for photobiological safety of lamps and lamp systems; the IEC page explicitly includes LED sources while excluding lasers.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use `LM-79` for finished SSL product optical/electrical measurement framing, not source lifetime projection.
- Use `LM-80` plus `TM-21` language only when actual LED-source maintenance data and projection conditions are available.
- Use `IEC 62471` for photobiological-safety framing, not for luminous flux, CRI, thermal resistance, or medical efficacy.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove `L70`, `50,000 h`, `15 years`, field lifetime, thermal design success, or product reliability for any LED PCB.
- It does not prove an LED board is medical-device safe, FDA-cleared, automotive-qualified, or suitable for patient contact.
- It does not provide integrating-sphere setup details, goniophotometer procedure, tolerances, or pass/fail criteria.
