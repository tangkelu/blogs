# Canonical Summary

> Public IAQG, SAE, IPC Validation Services, and DLA records are strong enough to show that `qualification`, `listing`, `certification`, `validation`, `audit`, and `release authority` are different layers in hi-rel governance. They are not strong enough to prove that any specific `22-layer` supplier, lot, or board is approved, listed, qualified, certified, or release-authorized.

## Stable Facts

- A conservative `22-layer` rewrite may state that public governance ecosystems distinguish baseline `QMS`, audit process, certification-record visibility, qualification listing, and formal nonconformity documentation.
- Public `AS9100D` metadata supports a baseline `QMS` layer under customer and regulatory precedence, not supplier approval.
- Public `AS9101G` metadata supports an audit-process layer, not audit outcomes or approved status.
- Public `AS9131D` metadata supports contract-driven nonconformity-documentation vocabulary, not disposition or release authority.
- Public IAQG `OASIS` and certification pages support certification-record ecosystem vocabulary, not customer acceptance.
- Public IPC Validation Services pages support the idea that standards can coexist with gap analysis, validation, and listing ecosystems, but those ecosystems do not automatically qualify finished boards.
- Public `QPL` / `QML` pages support listing-scope vocabulary for products, sites, or organizations, but do not prove customer-program acceptance.
- Public DLA `QML/QPL/QBL` infrastructure and `MIL-PRF-31032` hierarchy support qualification-ecosystem vocabulary, not current listing status or release authority for a specific supplier.

## Conditions And Methods

- Use this card when a `22-layer` draft risks collapsing `certified`, `listed`, `validated`, `qualified`, `approved`, and `release-authorized` into one claim family.
- Pair this card with `facts/standards/22-layer-high-reliability-rewrite-guardrail.md`, `facts/standards/fai-and-aerospace-quality-workflow-metadata.md`, and `facts/standards/high-reliability-program-and-outgassing-metadata.md`.
- Prefer wording such as `publicly identifies an ecosystem`, `can sit in a listing framework`, `may require customer confirmation`, and `does not prove approval by itself`.
- Keep `QPL`, `QML`, `OASIS`, `certification`, and `MIL-PRF-31032` references attached to role and scope boundaries rather than supplier-status conclusions.

## Limits And Non-Claims

- This card does not prove that any supplier is `AS9100` certified, passed an `AS9101` audit, or has authority to release or disposition `22-layer` product.
- It does not prove that any IPC Validation Services, `QPL`, or `QML` record qualifies a finished board, its interconnect structure, or a customer-specific program.
- It does not prove current listing status, accepted audit outcomes, customer approval, or release authority under `MIL-PRF-31032`.
- It does not provide supplier-evidence, site-evidence, lot-evidence, or current certificate status.

## Open Questions

- Add a supplier-evidence layer only if future work truly needs manufacturer-specific certification or listing claims.
- Reassess `22-layer` readiness only after a separate supplier-evidence discipline exists.
