## Official Sources Checked Or Candidate Official Sources To Recover

### Checked official / durable sources

1. ICC Incoterms rules
   - URL: https://iccwbo.org/business-solutions/incoterms-rules/
   - confirmed support:
     - Incoterms are standardized B2B trade terms
     - ICC states the current version is `Incoterms 2020`, entered into force on `January 1, 2020`
     - safe use is cost/task/risk allocation only, not fab-time or customs-clearance certainty

2. U.S. CBP Basic Importing and Exporting
   - URL: https://www.cbp.gov/trade/basic-import-export
   - confirmed support:
     - CBP treats duties, taxes, fees, and import-process requirements as live import-governance topics
     - Section 321 / de minimis is conditional, not a universal exemption

3. U.S. CBP Tips for New Importers and Exporters
   - URL: https://www.cbp.gov/trade/basic-import-export/importer-exporter-tips
   - confirmed support:
     - importer of record remains responsible for entry correctness and applicable duties, taxes, and fees even when using a broker
     - CBP examination can create importer-borne preparation / exam expense

4. DHL customs duties and taxes guide
   - URL: https://www.dhl.com/us-en/home/express/shipping-and-tracking/customs/customs-clearance/customs-duties-and-taxes.html
   - confirmed support:
     - most international shipments are subject to customs duties and taxes
     - complete and accurate goods descriptions and documents are required for clearance framing
     - safe use is customs-document and landed-cost governance, not PCB-specific tariff rates

5. DHL customs clearance documents guide
   - URL: https://www.dhl.com/us-en/home/express/shipping-and-tracking/customs/customs-clearance/customs-clearance-documents.html
   - confirmed support:
     - commercial invoice is the primary customs document
     - vague descriptions like `parts` or `samples` are not acceptable
     - incorrect or missing documents can delay, fine, return, or destroy shipments

6. FedEx international transit notes
   - URL: https://www.fedex.com/en-us/please_notes/wgrt/us/us/pn_expintlresults.html
   - confirmed support:
     - transit-time displays assume no clearance delays
     - displayed timing depends on declared value, description, packaging, address, dimensions, weight, and ship date
     - safe use is shipping-estimate caveats, not manufacturing lead-time support

7. IPC standards revision table
   - URL: https://www.electronics.org/ipc-document-revision-table
   - confirmed support:
     - current public standards metadata can be checked here
     - supports revision-status handling for IPC finish standards and related metadata only

8. IPC Validation Services program page
   - URL: https://www.ipc.org/standards/ipc-validation-services
   - confirmed support:
     - QPL/QML are process or product-listing frameworks under IPC validation services
     - safe use is standards-governance framing, not finished-board approval or supplier ranking

9. IPC-4101 QPL page
   - URL: https://www.electronics.org/validation-services-qualified-products-list-qpl-ipc-4101
   - confirmed support:
     - QPL scope is base materials and listed sites/products against IPC-4101
     - safe use is laminate/prepreg qualification metadata only
     - do not turn this into proof that a PCB factory or blog-draft supplier is qualified as a finished-board source

10. Peters solder-resist product portfolio
    - URL: https://peters.de/elpepcb-cats-2/elpepcb-solder-resists/
    - confirmed support:
      - official vendor-level confirmation that colored solder-resist families exist across specific product lines
      - supports category existence and some product-family color availability
      - does not by itself authorize broad board-level optical or application claims

11. Peters ELPEPCB product overview
    - URL: https://peters.de/elpepcb/
    - confirmed support:
      - official color/product examples include black, white, blue, and green in named coating families
      - some products explicitly mention reflectivity, yellowing resistance, color resistance after lead-free reflow, or thick-copper top-coat suitability
      - safe use requires tying any performance statement to the exact product line rather than `white FR4` or `black FR4` generally

12. Peters LED optical requirements paper
    - URL: https://peters.de/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/ref-166e_000_SL_LPISM_VU_for_LEDs.pdf
    - confirmed support:
      - white solder resist for LED use needs exact reflectivity / yellowing / color-stability context
      - this supports a boundary: `white` is not a sufficient technical description by itself
      - does not authorize generic LED-efficiency claims for all white solder masks
