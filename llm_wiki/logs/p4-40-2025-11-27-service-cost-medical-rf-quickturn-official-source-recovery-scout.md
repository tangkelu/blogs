# Safe Reuse Classes

- Definitions and taxonomy:
  prototype, quick-turn, NPI, small-batch, mass production, turnkey, one-stop, HDI, impedance-controlled PCB, flex, rigid-flex, MCPCB, Rogers laminate family framing.
- Workflow and intake vocabulary:
  Gerber / ODB++ / IPC-2581 intake, DFM/DFT review, stackup review, sourcing review, test planning, traceability, box-build handoff.
- Material-parameter examples only when exact-product scope is preserved:
  Ventec IMS cards and Rogers product cards.
- Boundary statements:
  advanced boards need engineering review; medical wording must stay at documentation/traceability level; impedance needs verification, not just intent; bend guidance depends on thickness/layer/bend mode.
- Standards metadata only:
  IPC flex/rigid-flex metadata, FDA traceability/documentation vocabulary, HDI microvia definitions.

# Blocked Claim Classes

- Unsupported numeric claims:
  prices, savings, cost rankings, cost percentages, lead times, MOQ, quantity buckets, yield, defect rate, reliability life, thermal resistance, RF/electrical performance, medical qualification depth, capability ceilings.
- Unsupported supplier capability claims:
  “all major PCB types on one fast-turn platform”, “stable mass production”, “production-grade lines”, “high-yield repeatable production”, “same partner handles all builds with no disruption”.
- Unsupported certification / regulatory claims:
  ISO 13485, medical-grade, FDA relevance beyond documentation vocabulary, cleanroom / sterilization / patient-contact / device-release claims.
- Unsupported performance-outcome claims:
  guaranteed signal integrity, EMI compliance, field reliability, predictable quality, reduced hidden cost, shorter cycles, better yields, repeatable RF performance.
- Unsupported comparative claims:
  turnkey or one-stop is better than multi-supplier by default; Rogers is better than FR-4 in finished-board outcome terms; aluminum PCB is inherently compliant for medical or mains-safety contexts.
