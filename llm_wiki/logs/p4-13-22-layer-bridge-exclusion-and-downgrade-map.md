## Control outcome

A compliant next pass may still say that 22-layer boards often appear in mission-sensitive or regulated programs, that higher layer counts can increase lamination, plating, registration, and verification difficulty, and that such programs may involve stronger documentation, traceability, inspection, or first-build workflow than ordinary commercial work. It may reference `IPC-6012`, addendum families, `IPC-A-600`, `AS9102`, or similar standards only as governance/context vocabulary.

A compliant next pass must not say public numeric `Class 3` or `Class 3A` thresholds, reconstructed acceptance tables, universal coupon counts or sampling plans, outgassing acceptance numerics, accepted qualification paths, release-authority implications, or supplier-specific claims that HILPCB complies with, is certified to, is qualified under, or routinely delivers these hi-rel states without separate supplier evidence.
