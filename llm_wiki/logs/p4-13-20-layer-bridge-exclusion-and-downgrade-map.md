## Control Outcome

A compliant next pass may still say:

- Dense 20-layer designs can push planning toward HDI or build-up-oriented context.
- Stacked-microvia and sequential-lamination discussion is more process-sensitive and reliability-sensitive than simpler multilayer framing.
- Public sources can support guarded material-family context and workflow-level evaluation language.

A compliant next pass must not say:

- Specific geometry, registration, via, stack-height, lamination-count, or impedance numerics.
- `IST`, `TM-650`, `qualification`, `screening`, or `reliable` language as if it proves thresholds, pass/fail rules, or approval status.
- HIL-specific capability, process-control, compliance, lead-time, yield, or production-scale claims without separate dated supplier evidence.
- Off-scope substrate or fine-line bridge language used to inflate the rigid 20-layer proof chain.
