## Safe Reuse Classes

- service taxonomy labels:
  `prototype`, `quick-turn`, `small-batch`, `mass production`, `turnkey`, `one-stop`, `consignment`, `component sourcing`, `box build`, `rework`, `repair`, `redesign`
- process-route distinctions:
  build-stage vs schedule posture, sourcing gate vs production gate, fabrication vs assembly vs integration
- supplier-evaluation framing without rankings:
  ask about process fit, traceability, review flow, documentation scope, and escalation path
- commercial-intent framing without numbers:
  cost drivers, quote inputs, tradeoff factors, total-cost considerations, sourcing risk categories
- medical and regulated framing at boundary level only:
  traceability, documentation, process control, release-gate sensitivity
- reverse-engineering / copying / cloning topics as demand inventory only:
  legal-review-needed service intent and deliverable categories, without legal conclusions or capability proof

## Blocked Claim Classes

- dynamic commercial numerics:
  unit price, total price, cost uplift, cost savings percentage, benchmark tables, MOQ, yield, scrap, DPPM, throughput
- dynamic schedule claims:
  same-day, 24-hour, 48-hour, rush windows, standard lead-time tables, delivery promises, shipping time promises
- supplier proof:
  approved supplier status, qualification status, transferable factory capability proof, accepted-lot implications, customer-trust proof
- certification and compliance proof:
  active certification claims, medical/automotive/aerospace qualification claims, regulatory readiness claims, audit status claims
- capability and equipment proof:
  exact process windows, advanced-material readiness, hidden-joint inspection coverage, factory capacity, staffing scale, automation level
- legal and ethical conclusions:
  board-copying legality, reverse-engineering permissibility, IP-safe cloning claims
- outcome guarantees:
  quality improvement, reliability improvement, cost reduction, schedule certainty, risk elimination, seamless scaling
