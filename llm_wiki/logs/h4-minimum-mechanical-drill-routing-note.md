## Current Blocker Shape

The current blocker shape for `minimum mechanical drill` is stable and unchanged:

- the corpus has drill/process framing, but not acceptable shared reusable numeric authority
- internal drilling and advanced-manufacturing pages help split mechanical drilling from laser drilling and other hole families, but they do not by themselves authorize transferable shared numeric recovery
- supplier-scoped proof or marketing language can mention drill capability, but it remains supplier-scoped and non-transferable by default
- standards-threshold pages, clause-family visibility, or hierarchy metadata may mention drilling-related criteria context, but they do not publish a safe shared reusable `minimum mechanical drill` number for this repo
- routing-rule summaries and derivative tables often collapse stack difficulty, hole quality, and shop capability into one unsupported minimum

The key split that must remain intact:

- `minimum mechanical drill` is a separate claim family from `minimum laser via`
- it is also separate from standards-threshold handling
- it is also separate from supplier-scoped dated proof

## Acceptable Authority Requirement

`Minimum mechanical drill` should be treated as shared reusable only if acceptable authority actually exists.

Minimum requirement:

- a primary source must expose the parameter directly as a minimum mechanical-drill field
- the scope must be explicit enough to show whether the number is shared reusable, supplier-scoped, process-scoped, product-scoped, or lot/build-scoped
- the source must distinguish ordinary mechanical through-hole drilling from laser-via, HDI build-up, backdrill, or other adjacent hole families
- the source must be strong enough that the number is not being reconstructed from summaries, routing heuristics, standards hierarchy, or marketing prose

Not acceptable as shared reusable numeric authority:

- supplier-scoped proof pages
- dated supplier capability records used outside their supplier scope
- standards-threshold pages
- clause-family summaries
- TOCs or hierarchy pages
- marketing summaries
- derivative comparison tables
- generic process-overview pages without direct parameter authority

If a future source is supplier-scoped and dated, it may support a narrow supplier-only handling lane.

It still does not become shared reusable numeric authority by default.

## Default Refusal Posture

If acceptable authority is missing, default posture is:

- refuse the exact `minimum mechanical drill` number
- keep only non-numeric through-hole difficulty or manufacturability context
- preserve the split between mechanical drilling and laser-via capability
- route supplier-specific drill-capability claims away from shared reusable handling
- reject standards-threshold pages, hierarchy pages, and marketing summaries as numeric basis

Default refusal wording should bias toward:

- `mechanical drilling capability depends on source-specific scope`
- `public reusable minimum drill authority is not yet established`
- `non-numeric manufacturability context may be kept, but the numeric minimum remains blocked`

## Non-Reuse Boundary

This note fixes the non-reuse boundary for `minimum mechanical drill`.

The following do not qualify as shared reusable numeric authority:

- supplier-scoped proof

- addendum or clause-family summaries

These sources may still help with:

## Routing Use Rule

When a draft asks for `minimum mechanical drill`, the controller should decide only:

- which branch is creating demand
- whether the claim is really about mechanical drilling rather than laser via or standards-threshold logic
- whether acceptable direct authority exists
- whether the claim must remain non-numeric or be routed to a narrower supplier-scoped lane

This note is routing only.

It is not numeric recovery.
