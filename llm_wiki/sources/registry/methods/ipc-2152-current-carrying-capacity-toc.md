# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- official IPC-2152 standard identity for current-carrying capacity in printed board design
- section-level evidence that the standard treats conductor sizing, conductor width, conductor thickness, copper weight, conductor temperature rise, vias, copper planes, and environment as distinct factors
- section-level evidence that the standard includes dedicated conductor sizing charts rather than a one-line formula rule

## Why It Matters

- Gives `llm_wiki` a primary standards-owner anchor that current-driven PCB consequences belong to a separate conductor-sizing lane, not to the basic `watts-to-amps` formula lane.
- Supports the narrow correction that drafts should not treat `amps from watts` alone as sufficient for board-level width or copper decisions.
