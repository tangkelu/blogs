# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- official FAA operational guidance for `radio altimeter`, also called `radar altimeter` or `RADALT`
- direct height-above-terrain / obstacle-clearance measurement role
- dependence of other aircraft systems and displays on radio-altimeter outputs
- susceptibility to radio-frequency interference in C-band context

## Why It Matters

- It supports framing radio-altimeter boards as safety-sensitive RF/interface subsystems without pretending the PCB itself is the certified avionics product.
