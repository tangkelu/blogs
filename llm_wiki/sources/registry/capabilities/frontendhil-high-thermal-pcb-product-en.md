# Source Summary

HIL High Thermal Conductivity PCB product specifications for LEDs and power electronics.

## Key Capabilities

### Material Options
- **Metal Core (MCPCB)**:
  - Aluminum: Cost-effective LED/power
  - Copper: Higher conductivity
  - System conductivity: 1–3 W/m·K (dielectric-limited)
- **Ceramic**:
  - Alumina (Al2O3): ~18–25 W/m·K
  - Aluminum Nitride (AlN): ~150–170 W/m·K
  - CTE-matched to silicon

### Dielectric
- **Thickness**: 75–150 μm (dominates thermal resistance)
- **Tolerance**: ±10%
- **Optimization**: Resin/filler ratio can reduce Rth by 20–30%

### Thermal Vias
- **Diameter**: Ø0.30–0.50 mm
- **Pitch**: 1.0–1.5 mm
- **Fill**: Copper-filled (10–20× better than resin-filled)

### Heavy Copper
- **For lateral spreading**: ≥3 oz

### Layer Count
- **MCPCB**: 1–4 layers (standard), up to 40+ (advanced)
- **Thermal FR-4 hybrids**: Up to 40+ layers

### Surface Quality
- **Roughness (Ra)**: ≤3 μm
- **Local flatness**: ±25 μm over pad fields (for TIM)

### Validation
- **Thermal cycling**: −40°C ↔ +125°C, 500–1,000 cycles
- **Thermal shock**: −40°C ↔ +150°C
- **Interface integrity**: Microsection analysis

### Applications
- LED lighting
- Power conversion
- RF PA modules
- High-power semiconductors

### Design Support
- FEA-backed thermal modeling
- CTE-aware footprint design
- Thermal via optimization
