# Source Summary

HIL Teflon/PTFE PCB manufacturing for RF, microwave, and mmWave applications.

## Key Capabilities

### Material Properties
- **Dielectric loss (Df)**: 0.0009–0.0015 @ 10 GHz
- **Dielectric constant (Dk)**: 2.1–2.3 (stable)
- **Frequency range**: Up to 40+ GHz

### Layer Count
- **Standard**: 1–20 layers
- **Advanced**: Up to 40+ layers

### Hybrid Stackups
- **Cost optimization**: PTFE on RF layers, FR-4 on inner planes
- **Cost reduction**: 30–50% vs full PTFE

### Impedance Control
- **Tolerance**: ±3–5%
- **Validation**: TDR and VNA S-parameters

### Manufacturing Challenges & Solutions
- **Challenge**: PTFE low surface energy, soft mechanical properties
- **Solution**: Plasma activation and controlled oxidation for adhesion
- **Challenge**: Bonding, hole-wall integrity
- **Solution**: High-temperature prepregs with CTE-matched glass fabrics
- **Challenge**: Drill heat causing delamination
- **Solution**: Laser or micro-drilling at reduced chip-load

### Copper
- **Type**: Rolled/VLP (Very Low Profile)
- **Benefit**: ~10–25% lower conductor loss

### Microvias
- **Technology**: UV-laser
- **Size**: 75–100 μm

### Backdrill
- **Purpose**: Remove resonant stubs for 25+ Gbps channels
- **Residual stub**: <10 mil

### Validation
- **TDR**: ±3–5% impedance verification
- **VNA**: S-parameters to 40 GHz
- **Coupon-based**: Correlation with field-solver models

### Lamination
- **Method**: Staged temperature/pressure
- **Plasma**: Dual-stage activation

### Board Thickness
- **Standard**: 0.20–3.20 mm
- **Advanced**: 0.10–6.00 mm

### Standards
- IPC-2221
- IPC-4103
- IPC-6018-style workflows

### Applications
- RF circuits
- Microwave (mmWave)
- High-frequency designs
- Low-loss signal chains

### Cost Strategy
- Hybrid stackups for cost/performance balance
- Teflon only where RF performance required
