# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- wastewater collection and treatment asset context
- remote sensors for flow, level, rainfall, and operating-status monitoring
- RTDSS, SCADA, and automated controls such as gates, weirs, valves, and pumps

## Why It Matters

- Gives `water-treatment` rewrites a real wastewater-operations context instead of a broad waterproof-electronics posture.
- Supports the split between sensing, communications, and pump/valve/actuator control in industrial water systems.

## Extraction Notes

- Safe for wording that wastewater systems use remote sensors and centralized data handling for real-time operating insight.
- Safe for wording that RTDSS works with SCADA and that automated controls can include gates, weirs, valves, and pumps.
- Do not use for ROI, overflow reduction, cost savings, or field-performance proof in PCB articles.
