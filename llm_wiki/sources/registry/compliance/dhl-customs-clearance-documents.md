# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- Commercial invoice as the primary customs document for international trade.
- Required shipment-description fields such as seller, buyer, clear goods description, value, currency, origin, tariff code, quantity, and weight.
- DHL's warning that vague descriptions such as `parts` or `samples` are not acceptable.
- Document gaps can cause delay, fines, return, or destruction of shipments.

## Why It Matters

- PCB and PCBA shipping articles can safely discuss document quality and classification readiness, but should not promise customs speed.
