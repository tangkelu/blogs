# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- Official IPC Validation Services page for the `Qualified Manufacturing Companies (QML) J-STD-001S Space/Military Addendum`
- Public framing that `J-STD-001S` supplements identified `IPC J-STD-001` requirements for soldered electrical and electronic assemblies in space and military applications
- Public statement that this `QML` is used to qualify `EMS` and `OEM` providers' assembly processes to IPC standards, with listed company/class/process metadata

## Why It Matters

- It sharpens the current hi-rel governance boundary by showing that IPC also uses `QML` language at the assembly-process layer for `EMS/OEM` providers under `J-STD-001S`, which is narrower and different from organization-level trusted-source `IPC-1791` framing and from bare-board qualification framing.

## Extraction Notes

- Safe for `J-STD-001S`, `IPC-A-610`, `EMS/OEM`, assembly-process qualification-listing, and class/process-scope vocabulary
- Do not infer bare-board qualification, finished-board qualification, supplier approval, customer acceptance, release authority, or multilayer rigid-board capability from this page alone
