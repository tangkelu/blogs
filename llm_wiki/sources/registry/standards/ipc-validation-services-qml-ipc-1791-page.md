# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- Official IPC Validation Services page for the `Qualified Manufacturing Companies (QML) IPC-1791`
- Public framing that `IPC-1791` sets trusted electronic designer, fabricator, and assembler requirements
- Public evidence that IPC maintains an organization-level `QML` listing for companies seeking trusted-source standing in markets requiring high integrity assurance

## Why It Matters

- It sharpens the current hi-rel governance boundary by showing that `QML` can sit at the organization and role level for trusted-source programs, rather than being read as finished-board qualification or customer acceptance.

## Extraction Notes

- Safe for `IPC-1791`, `trusted source`, organization-level `designer / fabricator / assembler` QML scope, and integrity-assurance market vocabulary
- Do not infer supplier approval, finished-board qualification, release authority, or acceptability for any specific `22-layer` program from this page alone
