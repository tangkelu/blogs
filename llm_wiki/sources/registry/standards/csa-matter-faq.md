# Source Summary

## What It Covers

- CSA FAQ-level Matter identity as an open smart-home protocol
- Matter setup and transport vocabulary: Bluetooth LE for setup, Wi-Fi, Thread, and Ethernet for connecting devices
- Device-category, certification, Matter badge, multi-admin, bridge, Distributed Compliance Ledger, and Zigbee interaction context

## Why It Matters

- Supports `tmps/2025.11.3/en/smart-home-diy-automation.md` and the Matter-over-Thread portions of `esp32-projects.md`.
- Provides standards-owner boundaries for smart-home interoperability claims.

## Extraction Notes

- Safe to cite Matter as an open smart-home protocol running over existing technologies, and to cite CSA bridge language for Zigbee or other protocols participating via bridges.
- Safe to state CSA distinguishes Matter and Zigbee as different protocols and says there is no native interoperability between Matter and Zigbee devices.
- Do not use this page alone to claim any specific device, board, module, controller, ecosystem, or customer product is Matter-certified or interoperable.
- Do not turn CSA FAQ language into performance, latency, privacy guarantee, local-control guarantee, cybersecurity guarantee, or ecosystem recommendation claims.

## Refresh Notes

- Refresh before citing supported device categories, certification requirements, DCL rules, bridge status, Matter version status, or current platform/vendor support.
