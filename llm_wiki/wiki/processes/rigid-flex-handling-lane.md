# Definition

> Rigid-flex handling is the assembly-stage lane that keeps carrier support, stiffeners, bend-sensitive transition review, and documentation discipline aligned while the board moves through build and test.

## Routing Guidance

- Use this page for assembly-stage rigid-flex handling, carrier support, stiffener use, and bend-zone transition review.
- Route bend-radius and bend-ratio questions here only as design-guide context, not as acceptance thresholds.
- Route medical, wearable, coating, THT, and BGA language here only when it is about manufacturing-control context, not compliance proof.
- Route product qualification, qualification coverage, and release authority claims elsewhere.

## Why This Topic Matters

- Rigid-flex content becomes unsafe when bend guidance is mixed with generic flex claims or with finish / coating topics that do not actually govern handling.
- The current corpus already supports bend-guidance scope, stackup posture, and assembly-stage handling.
- This page gives a single place to keep rigid-flex discussion at manufacturing-control level.

## Stable Facts

- Rigid-flex handling is tied to stackup, bend zone, carrier tooling, and assembly-stage support.
- Static bend and dynamic flex are different cases and should not be collapsed into one claim.
- Carrier, stiffener, placement, and transition review all affect handling discipline.
- Bend ratios remain design-guide context and do not become universal thresholds through repetition.
- Medical or wearable packaging pressure can raise handling constraints, but it does not change the bend-guidance boundary.
- Medical or wearable context can increase handling pressure, but it does not change the underlying bend-guidance boundary.

## Engineering Boundaries

- Do not turn bend guidance into a universal acceptance threshold.
- Keep handling, support tooling, and bend-zone review separate from material identity.
- Do not imply flex-only rules automatically apply to rigid-flex builds.
- Do not convert assembly-stage handling into compliance, qualification, or release claims.
- Do not turn medical-adjacent manufacturing-control language into device-approval or safety proof.

## Blocked Claims

- flex-life guarantees
- universal handling rules
- compliance or qualification claims

## Common Misreadings

- Carrier support is not a bend-life guarantee.
- Stiffener use is not a proof of reliability.
- Assembly-stage handling is not the same as finished-product qualification.
- Bend guidance is not a promise of cycle life.
- Medical-adjacent context is not compliance proof.

## Must Refresh Before Publishing

- Any exact bend radius, bend ratio, or cycle-life number
- Any customer-specific handling or acceptance rule
- Any claim that would move this page from manufacturing-control context into qualification or compliance language
