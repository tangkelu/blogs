## Module: Comparison / Decision framing (enable when keyword is “X vs Y”)

When this module is enabled, the article must be decision-oriented and include:

### Required tables
1) A comparison table:
`Factor | Option A | Option B | Best when | Trade-off`
- At least 8 rows.
- Make trade-offs explicit (do not claim one option is always better).

2) A compact decision matrix:
`Priority | Best choice | Why`
- 4–6 rows (performance, cost, lead time, reliability, manufacturability).

### Required content
- A “How to choose …” section with 6–10 rules written as:
  - **If you prioritize X, choose Y; otherwise choose Z (trade-off).**
- Include at least 2 “boundary/exception” callouts (when the usual rule does not apply).
- FAQ must include at least 4 long-tail modifiers: cost / lead time / testing / acceptance criteria.

### Tone guardrails
- No hype. Use engineering language: constraints, risks, verification, pass criteria.
- If a number is uncertain, say “confirm during DFM/quote” rather than inventing.
